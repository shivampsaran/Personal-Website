Emory University, Class of 2023

This past spring, I graduated from Emory University with a B.S. in Quantiative Sciences and a Minor in Physics. I was awarded Summa Cum Laude (Highest Honors) for the completion of my undergraduate honors thesis, which explored the landscape of America's bail system and introduced a novel machine learning approach for measuring racial and ethnic disparities in bail setting.

Surveillance Technology Oversight Project

During the Fall 2022 semester, I had the oppurtunity to work with the Surveillance Technology Oversigh Project (S.T.O.P) as a research intern. I researched a number of issues related to the unjust uses of surveillance technologies by law enforcement agencies, including the secretive system of police foundations and how fusion centers target undocumented immigrants.

The Roosevelt Network

The Roosevelt Network is an organization dedicated to developing young progressives into future policymakers and champions of justice. Throughout my undergraduate years, I was involved with the network through several capacities. Currently, I'm in the inaugral cohort of their Young Professionals Program.

National Policy Expo, Director

As Director of the Roosevelt Network's National Policy Expo, I oversaw the planning, promotion, and all logistics of the inaugral event.

Southern Regional Coordinator / Chapter Organizer

As a member of the networks National Student Leadership Team, I supported the policy work of Roosevelt chapters in the Southern Region through strategic planning and operational support.

2020 Summer Fellow

As a 2020 Summer Fellow, I developed an issue brief detailing the history of employment programs for Persons with Disabilities (PWDs) in California and proposed a solution to better integrate them into the workforce. You can view the brief here.

American Bar Association

During the Spring 2022 semester, I was a public interest intern within the Center for Pro Bono at the American Bar Association. Under the mentorship of Marissa LaVette & Cheryl Zalenski, I researched Access to Justice data, as well as public interest programs in various jurisdictions and developed resources containing the results of such research.
